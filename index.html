Observations

  • Police cars have their lights on at all times. Makes them easier to see. If they want to pull you over they will turn on their sirens.
  • In the urban areas, there are almost always separate bike lanes separated by a little garden strip. The taxis drive in these sometimes (not sure if normal cars can do that).
  • There may be squatter or even western toilets available, but never assume there will be toilet paper!
  • The communities are much richer with life (more people outside) and almost everything you need is within a few blocks away.
  • Streets on-average are pretty dirty. Sewage issues. But the green effort is more apparent than in the USA, for example you need to pay for plastic bags. Also, the more environmentally damaging vehicles can only drive at night.
  • The pollution in Taiyuan was so bad sometimes it felt like you were breathing in "heavy" air. I got kinda sick from it...kind of like a light flu.
  • Some Chinese versions of American products I saw: Budweiser, PBR, Pizza Hut, McDonald's. The beer cans are the old 70's tab tear-off kind.
  • In advertising, there seems to be a sort of perceived American superiority. Almost more common to see American models rather than Asian models...for Asian products!
  • Almost no foreigners in Taiyuan! Saw less than 5 in about a week of being there.
  • It's common to wear indoor sandals when indoors and leave your outside shoes in the front.
  • Seeing people wearing bike helmets was extremely rare. Even on the dangerous busy streets.
  • Sometimes there will be gender-specific bathrooms on different floors. Like the 1st floor will have only a woman's bathroom, and the 2nd floor will have only the men's.
  • It's very polite to allow the guests to sleep in the master bedroom, while the hosts sleep in the inferior bedroom (happened to us twice).
  • In fast-food restaurants (or any restaurant), you don't clean up after yourself when you are done eating. Just leave the mess there for a worker to bus your table. And no tips!
