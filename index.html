Observations

  • Police cars always have their lights on. If they want to pull you over they will turn on their sirens.
  • There are almost always separate bike lanes separated by a little garden strip. The taxis drive in these sometimes.
  • There may be squatter, or even western, toilets available, but never assume there will be toilet paper!
  • The communities are much richer with life and almost everything you need is within a few blocks away.
  • Streets on average are pretty dirty. But the green effort is more apparent, for example you need to pay for plastic bags. Also, the more environmentally damaging vehicals can only drive at night.
  • The pollution in Taiyuan was so bad sometimes it feels like you are breathing in "heavy" air. I got sort of sick from it...kind of like a light flu.
  • Some chinese versions of american products I saw: Budweiser, PBR, Pizza Hut, McDonalds. The beer cans are the old 70's tab tear-off kind.
  • In advertising, a sort of percieved American superiority. Almost more common to see american models rather than asian for asian products.
  • Almost no forieners in Taiyuan! Saw less than 5 in about a week of being there.
  • It's common to wear indoor sandals when indoors and leave your outside shoes in the front.
  • Seeing people use bike helmets was extremely rare. Even on the dangerous busy streets.
  • Sometimes there will be gender-specific bathrooms on different floors. Like the 1st floor will have only a woman's bathroom, and the 2nd floor will have only the men's.
  • It's very polite to allow the guest to sleep in the master bedroom.
  • In fast-food restauraunts (or any restauraunt), you don't clean up after yourself. Just leave the mess there for someone to bus your table. And no tips!
